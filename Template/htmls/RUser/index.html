Artificial Intelligence, Cybersecurity, E-Governance, and Smart Cities

Artificial intelligence (AI) has been identified as a critical technology of the Fourth Industrial Revolution (Industry 4.0) for protecting computer network systems against cyber-attacks, malware, phishing, damage, or illicit access. AI has the potential to strengthen the cyber capabilities and safety of nation-states, local governments, and non-state entities through e-Governance. Existing research provides a mixed association between AI, e-Governance, and cybersecurity; however, this relationship is believed to be context-specific.
